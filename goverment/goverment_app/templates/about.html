Government Schemes About

Government schemes refer to programs or initiatives launched by the government to provide benefits, services, or support to its citizens. Here are some examples of government schemes:
1. Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana: It is a financial inclusion program launched by the Indian government in 2014 to provide basic banking services to all citizens, especially those from low-income households.
2. National Health Protection Scheme: It is a flagship healthcare program of the Indian government launched in 2018 to provide health insurance coverage to over 100 million families.
3. Swachh Bharat Abhiyan: It is a cleanliness drive launched by the Indian government in 2014 to promote cleanliness, hygiene, and sanitation across the country.
4. Make in India: It is an initiative launched by the Indian government in 2014 to promote manufacturing and attract foreign investment to India.
5. National Rural Employment Guarantee Act: It is a social security scheme launched by the Indian government in 2005 to provide employment to rural households and alleviate poverty.
6. Digital India: It is an initiative launched by the Indian government in 2015 to promote digital infrastructure, digital literacy, and digital services across the country.
7. Clean Energy and Sustainable Development Goals: It is an initiative launched by the Indian government to promote the use of clean energy, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and achieve the sustainable development goals.
These are just a few examples of government schemes. Governments around the world launch various initiatives to promote economic development, social welfare, healthcare, education, and environmental protection, among others.
